Generations of Computers

First Generation
Electronic Device: Vacuum Tubes
Speed: Millisecond
Primary Memory: Magnetic Core Memory
Secondary Memory: Punched Cards, Paper Tape
Language: Machine Level
Cost: Very High ($2.5M)
Heat & Power: Very High
Capability: Very Less
Examples: UNIAC, ENIAC, EDSAC
Second Generation
Electronic Device: Transistors
Speed: Microsecond
Primary Memory: Magnetic Core Memory
Secondary Memory: Magnetic Tape, Drum
Language: Assembly + High Level
Cost: High ($250k)
Heat & Power: High
Capability: Less
Examples: IBM 1401, CDC 1604
Third Generation
Electronic Device: IC
Speed: Nanosecond
Primary Memory: Semiconductor Memory
Secondary Memory: Magnetic Disk + Tape
Language: High Level
Cost: High ($25k)
Heat & Power: Low
Capability: Moderate
Examples: IBM 360, IBM 370
Fourth Generation
Electronic Device: Microprocessor
Speed: Picosecond
Primary Memory: Semiconductor Memory
Secondary Memory: Magnetic + Optical Disks
Language: 4GL + High Level
Cost: Low ($2.5k)
Heat & Power: Very Low
Capability: High
Examples: IBM PC, Apple Macintosh
Fifth Generation
Electronic Device: Bio-Chips
Speed: LIPS, Femto+
Primary Memory: Unknown
Secondary Memory: New Storage Tech
Language: Natural Language
Cost: Unknown
Heat & Power: Unknown
Capability: Much Higher
Examples: Unknown
